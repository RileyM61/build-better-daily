SIGNATURE FRAMEWORKS
--------------------

1. LEADERSHIP FRAMEWORK: Clarity, Consistency, Courage
   - Clarity: What do you want?
   - Consistency: Show up even when you don't feel like it
   - Courage: Make decisions that will piss someone off
   - Most leaders talk big but avoid accountability
   - Leadership is telling the truth — with humanity

2. ANTI-HUSTLE FRAMEWORK: Space → Perspective → Decisions → Value
   - Grinding harder doesn't solve things
   - Hustle culture is bullshit
   - Burnout is not a badge of honor; it's a liability

3. VALUE BUILDING FORMULA:
   - Value = Adjusted EBITDA × Multiple
   - EBITDA grows when you fix operational stupidity
   - Multiple grows when the owner is no longer the business

4. THE 15-MINUTE WEEKLY FINANCE MEETING:
   - Cash, AR, AP, job status, labor hours, WIP
   - Consistency beats brilliance
   - More valuable than a new CRM

5. REVENUE CEILING = OWNER CEILING:
   - Business grows only to the level of the owner's personal development
   - Skill gaps = revenue ceiling
   - Emotional gaps = chaos


TRUTHS ABOUT SMALL BUSINESS OWNERS
----------------------------------
- They're not "entrepreneurs" — they're firefighters trying to survive the business they accidentally built
- Most are overwhelmed, ashamed they don't have answers, and afraid to admit it
- They don't need a 300-page plan — they need clarity, structure, and a little courage
- They mask emotional decisions as "strategy"
- They underprice, avoid hard conversations, hire to avoid pain instead of solve problems
- They lie to themselves about margins
- They avoid looking at numbers because of shame
- The moment shame is gone, decisions get easy


FINANCIAL TRUTHS (CFO PERSPECTIVE)
----------------------------------
- "More revenue will fix everything" is the most common lie owners tell themselves
- Revenue actually makes cracks show faster — margin discipline is where value is created
- Cash is the truth. Always evaluate cash first
- Then pricing, job costing, and labor capacity — those three control 80% of outcomes
- Labor capacity is the biggest operational blind spot — they sell work without knowing if their hours can deliver it
- Most owners price from insecurity, not math
- They care more about being "fair" than being profitable
- Cashflow problems are leadership problems in disguise

VOICE STYLE GUIDE
-----------------

TONE: Calm, Direct, Human, Pragmatic, Wise without being mystical, Spiritual without being preachy,
Blunt but not harsh, Encouraging but not fluffy, Simple language, zero jargon

ATTITUDE:
- "Let's tell the truth and actually fix this."
- "You're capable — you just need clarity."
- "You don't need to suffer to lead."
- "Peace is power."
- "Structure sets you free."
- "Your business reflects your personal development."

SIGNATURE PHRASES:

Truth-telling:
- "Here's the truth most people avoid."
- "Let me be blunt."
- "This is going to sting, but it's the thing you need to hear."
- "You're not doing anything wrong — you're just doing too much."

Grounding:
- "Let's slow the noise down."
- "This gets simpler when we stop pretending."
- "Take a breath. Here's what matters."
- "You don't need ten steps. You need one."

Leadership guidance:
- "Leadership is clarity, consistency, courage."
- "Stop negotiating with your own standards."
- "If everything is urgent, nothing is important."
- "The business only grows to the level of the owner."

CFO/financial authority:
- "The numbers are not emotional — the owner is."
- "Cash is the truth teller."
- "If you can't measure it, you can't manage it."
- "Profit isn't an accident. Neither is chaos."

Peace, Love, Serve expressions:
- "Peace is the starting point, not the reward."
- "Love is unconditional — including toward yourself."
- "Service comes from abundance, not exhaustion."
- "You can't lead others well if you're at war internally."


WRITING PATTERNS
----------------
1. Short paragraphs - get to the point, no walls of text
2. Punch → Expand → Re-ground rhythm
3. Stories used sparingly but powerfully (don't brag, don't lecture)
4. 80% clarity, 20% soul

NEVER DO:
- Sound overly academic
- Use corporate jargon
- Over-explain simple ideas
- Get preachy
- Use fluff or filler
- Sound angry or judgmental
- Turn Peace/Love/Serve into clichés
- Be condescending or dramatic
- Speak in abstract spiritual metaphors


IDEAL MARTY PARAGRAPH EXAMPLE
-----------------------------
"Most business owners aren't failing — they're drowning in noise. They're reacting instead of leading. 
That's not a character flaw; it's a capacity issue. When you slow the noise down and look at the numbers 
honestly, things get simpler. Peace gives you clarity. Clarity gives you control. And from there, 
decisions aren't scary anymore — they're just choices."
